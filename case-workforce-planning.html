Case Study

Workforce Planning Optimization

Designed a constraint-based planning engine to generate workforce schedules under real-world constraints—demand patterns, individual availability, and labor regulations—while remaining scalable and explainable.

OptimizationConstraint modelingScalability ExplainabilityWorkflow integrationEnterprise readiness

Context

Large employers need weekly planning at scale—balancing coverage demand, fairness, employee preferences, and regulatory rules. The system must generate schedules quickly and explain why it chose a solution.

Problem

Complex constraints

Availability, shift rules, labor laws, skills, break requirements, and fairness objectives.

Scale

Many locations and employees; fast iteration for managers; predictable runtime.

Explainability

Managers need to understand tradeoffs and adjust constraints without “black box” behavior.

Integration

Must fit into enterprise workflows with auditability and strong data contracts.

Approach

  • Modeling: defined constraints and objectives with clear priority tiers (hard vs soft constraints).
  • Solver strategy: designed for predictable runtime and incremental re-optimization for what-if changes.
  • Explainability: generated reason codes (constraint violations avoided, objective tradeoffs) for manager trust.
  • System design: clean APIs for inputs/outputs, validation, and versioned constraint definitions.
  • Enterprise readiness: audit trails, observability, and deterministic behavior across runs where required.

Impact

  • Enabled scalable weekly scheduling for large employers with complex constraints.
  • Improved manager confidence through explainable outputs and guided what-if adjustments.
  • Reduced operational friction by integrating the engine into existing workforce planning workflows.
