Applied AI in Enterprise Platforms: From Insight to Decision Systems

A practical perspective shaped by hands-on work across Workday Next and Thalamus—focused on embedding AI into workflows with strong data foundations, reliability, governance, and measurable business value.

AI as Systems Engineering

Artificial Intelligence delivers meaningful enterprise value only when it moves beyond experimentation and becomes embedded within operational systems. Across my work at Workday Next and Thalamus, I’ve focused on building AI capabilities that enhance decision-making at scale—combining data platforms, responsible design, and measurable outcomes.

Structured Decision Support — Thalamus

At Thalamus, AI was introduced into a high-trust domain: residency application screening and review. The challenge was improving fairness, consistency, and reviewer efficiency at scale—without removing human judgment.

Within the Cortex platform, AI pipelines standardized and contextualized unstructured applicant data. A representative example is transcript normalization, where AI techniques process transcripts with varying formats and grading systems to produce consistent representations that support equitable comparison.

The approach emphasizes responsible boundaries: AI aggregates and highlights information, humans retain final decision authority, and transparency and fairness are explicit system goals.

From Analytics to Optimization — Workday Next

At Workday Next, the focus was evolving enterprise analytics into decision-oriented workforce planning systems. Workforce planning requires forecasting, scenario modeling, and recommending actions aligned with business strategy—not only reporting.

Workforce planning unifies HR and financial data to model headcount and skills decisions against financial and strategic outcomes, enabling organizations to simulate scenarios, align talent strategy, and optimize allocation as conditions change.

Common Patterns That Make AI Work

Data foundations: governed pipelines, standardized schemas, and observable systems.

Workflow integration: AI improves decisions inside the user’s process, not as a side tool.

Human-in-the-loop: explainability and auditability are features, not afterthoughts.

Measurable impact: success is improved decisions and outcomes—not model accuracy alone.

My view: Enterprise AI creates lasting value when it improves real decisions while strengthening trust in the system making them.
