Case Study

Workday Analytics at Scale

Built and scaled analytics platform capabilities supporting enterprise HR/Finance workflows—balancing performance, governance, multi-tenant security, and reliability while scaling the organization and delivery cadence.

Multi-tenantPerformanceGovernance ObservabilityEnterprise reliabilityOrg scale

Context

Enterprise customers required trustworthy analytics embedded into business workflows—fast, secure, governed, and extensible. The platform needed to support growing customer scale, workload variety, and evolving reporting use cases.

Problem

Scale + performance

Support high query volumes and complex workloads without degrading customer experience.

Trust + governance

Ensure consistent definitions, security boundaries, and auditability in a multi-tenant environment.

Extensibility

Enable new reportable objects and analytics features without brittle coupling or long lead times.

Operational excellence

Raise reliability, observability, and incident response maturity as adoption grew.

Approach

  • Platform architecture: clear boundaries between operational systems and analytics surfaces; governed objects and semantics.
  • Performance strategy: indexing/partitioning, query optimization, caching layers, and workload-aware tuning.
  • Governance: strong security/authorization, data contracts, lineage/metadata, and consistent definitions across tenants.
  • Operational rigor: metrics-first engineering, SLOs, alerting, and incident playbooks to protect customer experience.
  • Org execution: built leadership bench, clarified ownership, and created repeatable delivery cadence as the org scaled.

Impact

  • Scaled analytics capabilities to serve thousands of enterprise customers with high reliability.
  • Improved platform robustness through observability and operational discipline as usage and complexity increased.
  • Enabled faster delivery of analytics features through clearer platform boundaries and reusable primitives.

What I’d do again

  • Invest early in governance + observability—these become compounding advantages at enterprise scale.
  • Keep platform primitives small and reusable; avoid one-off solutions that don’t generalize.
